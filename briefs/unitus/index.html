2. Technical and marketing stack

Meeting use: ask whether the infrastructure produces a weekly decision loop.

3. Sale-heavy merchandising signal

The public product feed showed 129 of 148 products with compare-at pricing above current price, about 87%, with a median discount around 35% among sale-marked products. This may be strategic, seasonal, inventory-driven, or reactive.

Meeting use: ask what the sale posture is meant to accomplish before diagnosing it.

4. John's planning language

Worthy Wins gives a useful meeting vocabulary: Worthy Win, TEA, objective, strategy, execution, reflection, and WLOPs. The strongest bridge is to ask what would make e-commerce a Worthy Win for Unitus this year, then translate the answer into a 90-day operating system.

Meeting use: use the language lightly. Keep the goal on alignment.

Standard or taste bar

The bar is disciplined and mission-aware. Unitus has a faith-driven brand with an active product catalog, athlete credibility, visible story, and technical tooling. WAWCO should avoid a generic Shopify audit posture and focus on the operating layer: definition of the win, weekly cadence, decision rights, shipped tests, and clean reflection.

Growth recommendations should protect the brand's Worthy Win. Revenue, ROAS, discounts, and activity volume should serve the stated win rather than become the win by default.

How we will work

  • Define the win first. Ask what Unitus needs e-commerce to accomplish this year and this quarter.
  • Map the current loop. Learn how paid traffic, creative, product priorities, offers, landing pages, email/SMS, analytics, and inventory decisions connect today.
  • Name the ownership gap. Find out who turns data into weekly actions, who decides what ships, and where work stalls.
  • Propose the smallest useful cadence. If there is fit, shape a 90-day system around one scorecard, one weekly decision meeting, one shipped test, and one WLOPs reflection loop.
  • Keep private context private. Do not cite secondhand budget signals, private Messages, or calendar details as client-facing facts.
