What's working

The visual language already has athletic energy: high-contrast photography, condensed all-caps type, dark overlays, and direct calls to action. The first-viewport homepage read is closer to a Nike campaign than a soft ministry page. The You Go First fundraising campaign hit its public goal framing: five retreats, tripled staff, doubled revenue, and a $65,000 target for 2026 expansion including devotionals, podcasts, events, conferences, and experiences.

The content engine is producing real proof: campus tour numbers, retreat testimonials, podcast episodes, professional guest appearances (Heart of Dating, Cleerely Stated), and a clear expansion arc from Fort Worth to a multi-campus national presence.

The immediate transition

The current social media vendor contract reportedly ends soon. Assumption to confirm That creates a narrow window: Noble needs continuity in posting, asset production, channel management, and approval workflows. The transition needs to happen without visible disruption to an audience that is actively growing through campus tours and podcast distribution.

This is where we start. The first month stabilizes the transition and maps the system. The second month builds the content infrastructure that can keep up with a live movement expanding to multiple campuses and cities.

The work below assumes Noble needs both a safe transition and a durable system. The transition sprint addresses the immediate timing pressure. The content system addresses the longer-term need: turning live events, podcast episodes, speaking clips, and field footage into a coordinated publishing rhythm across Noble, Nicoletta, and Call Her Holy.

Phase 1: 30-day transition sprint

Stabilize before momentum is lost

Goal: keep Noble visible and coherent when the current vendor contract ends. We manage the operational mapping so Nicoletta only needs to provide a few source materials and confirm a few decisions.

  • Account and asset intake across Noble, Nicoletta, and Call Her Holy
  • Channel map: what each account owns, what it links to, how they relate
  • Content pillars from Nicoletta's existing social direction document Assumption to confirm
  • Two-week emergency posting calendar
  • Clip and post production queue: podcast episodes, speaking footage, field content
  • Approval workflow for faith, body image, nutrition, and mental health language
  • Website and social routing cleanup list: account links, CTAs, official handles
Phase 2: 60-day content system

Turn the movement into a repeatable publishing rhythm

Goal: make social, podcast, speaking, workouts, campus tours, and retreats feed one another. The system should work whether Noble runs two events a month or ten.

  • Channel roles and cadence: what Noble posts vs. what Nicoletta posts vs. what Call Her Holy covers
  • Podcast and speaking clip pipeline: episode to social, long-form to short-form
  • Field content checklist for workouts, retreats, and campus events
  • Swipe post templates: encouraging, shareable, aligned with the visual direction
  • Testimonial capture and approval process
  • Event recap format: consistent enough to scale across campuses
  • Donor-facing proof bank: tour attendance, retreat outcomes, campus growth, campaign progress
  • Website alignment plan

Channel relationship question

How Noble, Nicoletta, and Call Her Holy relate

Public sources show three active brands with overlapping audiences and content themes. We do not assume they should be treated as one brand. The relationship between them, who coordinates, who posts where, and how they cross-promote, is a question for Nicoletta to answer before the content system can be built correctly.
