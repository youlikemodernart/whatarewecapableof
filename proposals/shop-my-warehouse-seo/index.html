Shop My Warehouse, and how it shows up in search.

We looked at Shop My Warehouse the way a search engine does: what we can already see from the outside, what a little read-only access would show, and a simple way to go further.

The short version

One-of-a-kind inventory

Shop My Warehouse sells one-of-a-kind pieces. Each is listed once, sells, and is gone, so most product pages are temporary. Search engines give the most weight to pages that stay put, which here means the collection and category pages rather than the individual products.

Layer one · from the outside

What we can already see

We looked only at what is public, the same view search engines have.

01

Sold pieces stay online

More than a quarter of the live product pages are items that already sold but are still online. They cannot be bought, so each should redirect to its collection when it sells, which keeps the search value it earned.

02

Collection pages have no text

Collection pages like Furniture, Lighting, and Vases keep the same address even as the products inside them change, so they are the best place to rank for a search like “vintage brass lamp.” Right now they have no description text for Google to read. This is the biggest opportunity.

03

Titles miss the search words

A page title is a top ranking signal, and the first words count most. Most titles read Name [VINTAGE] – Kaitlyn Wolfe, so the opening goes to the bracket and the brand instead of the words people search: the material, the era, the kind of object.

04

Photos have no alt text

Alt text is the short description attached to a photo that tells Google and screen readers what it shows. Every product image is missing it, so the photos cannot show in Google Images, where much vintage shopping starts.

05

Internal pages are visible in search

A few internal and quote-only collections, meant for staff or private sales, are open to Google alongside the public pages. They should be hidden so the public pages rank on their own.

06

What is already working

Much of the hard part is done. The store loads fast, the technical setup is solid, and it is already wired for the new AI shopping assistants. A strong base to build on.
