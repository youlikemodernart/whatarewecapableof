Why this exists

01

College is where many future founders decide what success means. Students are choosing career paths, mentors, habits, and communities before FDE is usually in the room. Campus clubs give the movement a presence at the point of identity formation.

02

FDE already has material that can carry a semester: the 8-week video series with J.D. Greear and Henry Kaestner, the language of faith and work, and the broader vision for entrepreneurs bringing their gifts to the altar. The campus format turns that material into a shared rhythm lived with peers.

03

Student leadership matters. A club run by students can feel like a movement on campus. A club delivered to students from the outside usually feels like programming. The proposed model gives students real responsibility, with coaching and guardrails around them.

04

Local church partnerships keep the work grounded in each city. The campus club becomes a bridge between students, churches, entrepreneurs, and the national FDE network.

05

The first year produces more than 10 clubs. It produces a repeatable launch playbook: how to choose a campus, recruit a student director, pair mentors, run gatherings, report to FDE, and hand the model forward.

What is already built

The proposal starts from existing FDE strengths: founder-level language, curriculum, a national entrepreneur network, local churches already connected to the movement, and a clear theological frame for work. The campus initiative builds the container where students can practice the mission.

What we would want to confirm

The plan depends on the first 5 campuses having the right conditions: a credible student director, a local church partner, at least one entrepreneur mentor, and enough institutional openness to gather weekly. A conversation would identify the first campuses, define the student director profile, and set the reporting cadence FDE leadership wants.

The program has three leader development layers and five recurring touchpoints. The layers define what each student leader receives. The touchpoints define the rhythm each campus lives inside.

Leader development framework

Layer 1

Content

FDE's 8-week video series with J.D. Greear and Henry Kaestner runs in community. Students watch, discuss, apply, and test the ideas against actual leadership decisions on campus.

Layer 2

Opportunity

Students lead weekly gatherings, semester events, and campus invitations. They do more than study faith-driven entrepreneurship. They practice leadership with real people in the room.

Layer 3

Coaching

Student directors connect bi-weekly with local and national Faith Driven Entrepreneurs: church FDE graduates, Christian business owners, and people who have carried faith through real operational pressure.

Operating principle

FDE provides the mission, the curriculum, the network, and the guardrails. Students carry the campus expression. That division protects the movement from becoming another centralized program while giving FDE the consistency it needs to scale responsibly.

Mission language

FDE's stated value is a "ruthless fervor to advance the mission for God's glory, not our own." The campus initiative follows that logic. Students learn to carry the mission from their own calling.

The first year is a build, launch, and scale cycle. It gives FDE a live campus program and a documented playbook the organization can own after the initial year.

Three-phase plan

Phase 1 / Now through December 2026

Recruit and build

Identify and confirm 5 campuses. Recruit and develop founding student directors. Establish local church partnerships. Run launch events and founding gatherings. Build spring curriculum cadence and coaching pairings. Send weekly reports to FDE leadership throughout the build.

Output: 5 clubs ready for the January 2027 spring semester.

Phase 2 / January 2027 spring semester

Launch

Five FDE campus clubs begin weekly gatherings. Bi-weekly coaching starts with all student directors. The national student cohort connects for the first time. Each campus runs a semester event. The next 5 campuses are identified and recruitment begins.

Output: 5 clubs fully operating and the second 5 in development.

Phase 3 / September 2027 fall semester

Scale

Ten clubs operate nationally. The first cohort of student directors has one full year of experience. The national cohort is active. The first yearly conference brings the campuses together. The launch playbook is documented and handed to FDE.

Output: 10 clubs operating and a repeatable process for the next 50 or 100.
