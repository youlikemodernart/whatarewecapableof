A course portal for the video breakouts, a live-session console for Zoom breaks, and a tracking surface for the 90 days after training.

Program

What the training already is

We spent two weeks with eight full session transcripts from the April 2026 cohort. Sales School is more structured and more deliberate than the email-and-PDF delivery surface suggests.

The program alternates between live Zoom sessions and self-paced video breakouts across two days. Jake facilitates the live sessions. Students watch Wistia videos during breaks. The live debriefs after each breakout are where the teaching happens: Jake calls on students by name, listens to their video takeaways, and layers stories and frameworks on top of their responses.

What the transcripts show

01

Day 1 builds the emotional base.

Day 1 is mental and emotional: build students up with choice, industry reality, and curiosity; deliver hard truths through the 12 Things You Need to Know About Sales; then rebuild with the Cycle of Achievement, Jake's centerpiece 10-step framework. Day 2 pivots to technical skills: communication, the approach, and objection handling.

02

The live teaching is reactive.

Jake has 19 named frameworks and a library of 45+ stories and analogies, each attached to a specific curriculum moment. He deploys them in response to what students say. He calls on raised hands, listens, validates, then adds a teaching layer. He never reads from slides.

03

Commitment is the close.

Session 8 brings the mental and technical threads together: 30 minutes of tactical Q&A, then the FIRST commitment talk: Future, Initiative, Resilience, Standards, Tenacity. Jake ends with his own origin story told in third person and revealed. The 30-60 day commitment is the throughline from Session 1, minute one.

04

Follow-up remains manual.

Jake contacts partner companies for approximately 90 days after each cohort, asking how students are performing. If a student fails, he considers it his fault. No app-based tracking exists. A prototype called Manus, built by Peter in roughly 5 hours, includes video hosting, a script tester, a Peak Performance Planner, and a 30-day post-field content shell. It was never launched.

05

The current delivery surface adds friction.

The path from entry to playback crosses seven surfaces: GoHighLevel preview, Firebase email HTML, email index, day section, breakout button, PDF index, and Wistia page. For post-session review, the extra steps are minor. During a timed Zoom break with 80 students, they compound.

Transfer

Austin's path into the room

Jake's instinct on the call was to sequence this: Austin runs the existing program first, holds metrics for 90 days, then the portal comes. The concern is real. He has paid for builds that didn't deliver, and he does not want a new system adding risk while a facilitator transition is already in progress.

Jake also wants what the portal would give him: app-based tracking, a cleaner delivery surface, and a system that does not require him to follow up with partner companies manually for 90 days after every cohort. Peter's Manus prototype was aimed at the same gap. Our recommendation is to build it alongside Austin's preparation, so both are ready at the same time. One transition for students, not two.

What transfers, what changes

Inherits

The curriculum and rhythm

The video library, six-breakout structure, major frameworks, exercises, assignments, two-day rhythm, alternating live-video-debrief format, Wistia content, and Miranda's logistics support.

Replaces

Jake's personal story library

Jack's story is Jake's origin: a 19-year-old whose car breaks down, who knocks 80 hours for $40 in his first week, finishes #17 in the world as a rookie, and comes back 8 more years. "His name wasn't Jack." That reveal is the program's emotional climax, and it only works as autobiography.

Develops

Austin's own story set

Austin needs his own versions of five recurring story types: the opening energy story, the hard-work-and-worth-it story, the resilience story, the commitment story, and the personal origin reveal. The frameworks transfer. The stories that carry conviction are personal.

Practices

The Socratic method

Austin develops the call-on and response pattern Jake uses in every Q&A debrief: calling on students by name, responding to their specific takeaways, and adding a relevant reframe or story in real time. He also develops the Session 8 commitment talk in his own voice.

Preparation path

Step 1

Observe

Watch all 8 session recordings. Study the curriculum map and content inventory. Map the emotional arc across both days. Identify which frameworks Austin can teach now and which need development.

Step 2

Shadow

Attend a live cohort as a participant. Study Jake's call-on and response patterns. Note the moments where his teaching pivots from framework to story.

Step 3

Co-facilitate

Run defined segments with Jake in the room. The homework debrief and a breakout debrief are natural starting points: structured enough to prepare for, reactive enough to test the Socratic method.

Step 4

Solo with support

Run a full two-day cohort with Jake available but not visible to students.

Step 5

Solo

The 90-day metrics window begins. Austin runs the program independently and holds the results.

Students open it, find their breakout, watch the videos, and return to Zoom. After training, the same address becomes the 90-day tracking surface. It ships when Austin does.

Live-break console

During Zoom breaks, students need to know what to watch, in what order, and when to return. The Today page shows the active breakout, the watch sequence, and the return time. The facilitator sets the active breakout from a control surface. One link shared in Zoom chat replaces the PDF lookup.

Post-training surface

The portal extends past the two-day session into the 90 days after. Students need video access for review, commitment tracking for 30, 60, or 90 days, the Carrot and the Stick exercise, a nightly script reading log, and a channel for check-ins that Jake currently handles manually. The training is 2 days. The behavior change happens over 90.
