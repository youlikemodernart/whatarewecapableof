Your first question · the studio site

What we can do on Cargo, and what needs the move

Some real wins do not need a new site, and we would do those first whatever you decide. Others are things Cargo cannot do, and they are the ones that move a site like this the most.

On Cargo now

Claim and build out the Google Business Profile, put a real address on the site, rewrite the page titles and the one shared description toward the words people search, and strip the nav, footer, and test pages out of what Google sees.

Needs the move

Per-page titles and descriptions, canonical tags, real headings, image alt text, structured data that tells Google the studio is a local design and build business, modern image formats and speed, a clean URL structure with redirect control, and changes made once across every page. Your second question · the studio site

How we protect rankings through the move

We treat a move as a controlled cutover, not a flip of a switch. The steps that hold onto the search value you have earned:

01

Inventory every current URL and decide, one by one: keep it, redirect it, or retire it.

02

Preserve the high-value URLs, and build a 301 redirect map for anything whose address changes, so old links and their search value carry forward.

03

Ship the new site with the full search layer already in place: titles, descriptions, canonicals, schema, headings, alt text, and a clean sitemap.

04

Keep the new site on hidden, no-index staging until it passes a page-by-page review.

05

Cut over, submit the new sitemap in Search Console, and keep the old links resolving through the redirects.

06

Watch Search Console coverage, rankings, and traffic after launch, and correct anything that moves.

The plan is discipline and measurement through the move. A clean cutover holds the equity, and we watch and fix rather than promise no movement. No one honest promises zero turbulence in a migration.

Your fourth question · both tracks

What we would measure in the first few months

The early work is mostly foundation and leading indicators, with the first real search readings starting once access is in.

Foundation

Access connected, the index cleaned up so junk pages drop and the real pages are submitted, the Business Profile built out, and coverage of titles, alt text, headings, and schema in place. On the studio site, the first service and location pages live and indexed. On the store, the sold-item lifecycle running and the top collections carrying real content.

The search read

Search Console impressions, clicks, click-through rate, and average position by page and query, plus organic sessions and the paths that lead to an inquiry or a sale once Analytics is connected.

The first stretch sets the baseline and fixes the structure, then starts measuring the movement. We do not promise ranking jumps before the access is in and Google has recrawled.
