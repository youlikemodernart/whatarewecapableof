Prepared for Noble · May 2026

Noble's public expansion, handoff intact.

Campus tours, Call Her Holy, retreats, and a founder audience are moving faster than one brand account can contain. The immediate need is a clean transition into a system that keeps up with the movement.

Summary

What we see from the outside

Noble is a 501(c)(3) fitness and faith ministry for women, founded by Nicoletta Bradley in Fort Worth. The public promise: women can find freedom from body image struggles, disordered relationships with food, comparison, and insecurity through faith-based fitness, teaching, community, retreats, and resources. The homepage language is "Made Noble. Live Free."

The public system is already active across multiple channels. Five patterns stood out.

Findings

01

Noble is in the field.

Spring 2026 campus tour attendance: over 700 at TCU, over 100 at Oklahoma University, over 50 at Palm Beach Atlantic, and 200+ expected at Baylor. Each event combines a workout with a live Call Her Holy podcast Q&A. TCU 360 reported Noble became an official campus club in fall 2024 with eight women on the leadership team, 15 ambassadors, and over 30 members.

Evidence: areyounoble.com/noble-x-call-her-holy-college-tours, TCU 360 Sep 2024.

02

The founder's audience is larger than the brand account.

Nicoletta's Instagram has 53K followers and 1,085 posts. Noble's brand account has 8,613 followers and 195 posts. Call Her Holy sits between them at 39K. On TikTok, Nicoletta has about 28K followers and 1.49M likes across 319 videos. The distribution surface is already multi-channel; any content system needs to decide what each channel owns.

Evidence: public Instagram and TikTok metadata, unauthenticated fetch.

03

Call Her Holy distributes.

191 Apple Podcast episodes since 2021. 4.7 rating from 452 ratings. The Call Her Holy website claims 1M+ downloads, 1,000+ five-star reviews, and 20K+ followers. The podcast already drives campus tour demand, speaking authority, and content themes that feed Noble's social surfaces.

Evidence: Apple Podcasts metadata, callherholy.com, callherholy.com/call-her-holy-x-noble.

04

The conversion system spans multiple tools.

Noble runs on Squarespace. Merch routes to Inksoft. Donations route through Classy. Nicoletta's personal site handles Bible study memberships through Stripe. Call Her Holy uses Donorbox and Shopify for its own merch. Tour check-in uses Google Forms. The current website navigation prioritizes About, Events, Testimonials, Donate, and Shop, but podcast, speaking, Bible study, and campus tour channels live on separate sites.

Evidence: areyounoble.com sitemap, nicoletta-bradley.com, callherholy.com.

05

The account map needs cleanup.

The Noble website's social icons route to Nicoletta's personal Instagram and TikTok accounts. The official Noble brand Instagram appears through the Call Her Holy x Noble page, not through Noble's own homepage. The giveaway terms instruct entrants to follow @AreYouNoble, but a public check of that handle returned a small unrelated profile.

Assumption to confirm.

What's working

The visual language already has athletic energy: high-contrast photography, condensed all-caps type, dark overlays, and direct calls to action. The first-viewport homepage read is closer to a Nike campaign than a soft ministry page. The You Go First fundraising campaign carried public goal framing: five retreats, tripled staff, doubled revenue, and a $65,000 target for 2026 expansion including devotionals, podcasts, events, conferences, and experiences.

The content engine is producing real proof: campus tour numbers, retreat testimonials, podcast episodes, professional guest appearances, and a clear expansion arc from Fort Worth to a multi-campus national presence.

The immediate transition

The current social media vendor contract reportedly ends soon. That creates a narrow window: Noble needs continuity in posting, asset production, channel management, and approval workflows. The transition needs to happen without visible disruption to an audience that is actively growing through campus tours and podcast distribution.

Confirm

Current vendor scope and exact end date are not confirmed. Austin said "about three weeks" on 2026-05-07.

This is where we start. The first month stabilizes the transition and maps the system. The second month builds the content infrastructure that can keep up with a live movement expanding to multiple campuses and cities.

Scope

Transition plus durable system

The work below assumes Noble needs both a safe transition and a durable system. The transition sprint addresses the immediate timing pressure. The content system addresses the longer-term need: turning live events, podcast episodes, speaking clips, and field footage into a coordinated publishing rhythm across Noble, Nicoletta, and Call Her Holy.
