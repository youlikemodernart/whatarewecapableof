Who this is for

You run a founder-led company. The knowledge that makes it work is spread across a few people, a dozen tools, and a lot of habit, and the real decisions still come back to you. Most of the leaders we work with have already tried the chatbots and the point automations. They help in places, and none of them runs the actual work.

How we work

We map the work before we change it.

01

Map

Inputs, handoffs, decisions, records, exceptions, and the owners who hold them. The result is your operating graph: the business made legible to itself.

02

Decide

Each process gets one answer: automate, assist, redesign, or stop. For anything genuinely new, one more question: build, buy, wait, or stop. Nothing is automated by default.

03

Build and hand off

We build only what earns its place, and hand it back: working systems, documentation, and an operating model your team can keep without us.

What a product cannot do

Software can run the work. It cannot tell you not to build it. Four things a product cannot give a founder, and we can:

  • A recommendation that might be "don't build." Build, buy, wait, or stop. We do not earn more when you build more.
  • Judgment accountable to a person. Every system gets a named owner, a review path, a failure path, and a clear line on what the AI is allowed to decide.
  • A system your team can keep. We leave documentation and a clearer operating model, not a dependency on us.
  • Advice that would not change if we had nothing to sell.

We refuse AI theater: demos, workshops, and tool lists that create motion without operating value.

Common questions

What if the answer is don't build?

We say so, and tell you why. We would rather lose the build than hand you a system you have to maintain forever.

Who owns the system after you leave?

You do. We design for handoff: documentation, a named internal owner, and an operating model your team understands.

What will you refuse to do?

Deceptive automation, surveillance-first tooling, and anything that quietly puts a machine in charge of a decision a person should own.

How will we know it helped?

We agree on that before we start: cleaner handoffs, faster cycles where speed matters, fewer guessed-at AI decisions, and knowledge your team can actually find.

What we leave behind

Judgment, documentation, and a system your team can keep.
