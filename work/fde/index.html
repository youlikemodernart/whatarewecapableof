What we see

The material is present: the Foundation Course, groups, podcast, related Faith Driven properties, church relationships, and a founder network.

The problem we see is activation. A new participant needs a clearer path into the material, and a student leader needs a model for turning the material into a durable campus practice.

What we are suggesting

Suggested move 01

Make the Foundation Course easier to enter

The course site is a proposed public front door. It separates the individual founder path from the church group path, gives the eight-week course a clear outline, and connects the course to groups, podcast episodes, founder stories, and Faith Driven family links.

Suggested move 02

Build a campus initiative around the same formation logic

The campus proposal turns the course, community, and mentor network into student-led clubs. The first year focuses on 5 clubs by January 2027, 10 clubs by September 2027, biweekly coaching, local church partnerships, national cohorts, and a launch playbook Faith Driven Entrepreneur owns.

Suggested move 03

Connect the two paths

The Foundation Course gives campus clubs a starting curriculum. Campus clubs give the course a repeatable student expression, with local churches and entrepreneurs helping carry the formation beyond a landing page.

What we could carry

Course site production

Finalize the public course experience: form destination, church inquiry flow, support copy, current stats, privacy language, QA, and handoff.

Campus initiative

Build the first-year campus plan: first 5 campus selection, student director profile, local church partner standard, entrepreneur mentor pool, reporting cadence, and launch playbook.

Connected path

Use the Foundation Course as the entry curriculum for campus clubs, then use the campus clubs as a repeatable distribution and formation model.

Decisions to make together

  • Which first 5 campuses have the right conditions: a credible student director, a local church partner, at least one entrepreneur mentor, and room to gather weekly.
  • Who should review the Campus Initiative proposal first, and what context should accompany it.
  • Where the Foundation Course signup and church inquiry forms should submit in production.
  • Which course-site support claims, current counts, and multilingual support language are approved for public copy.
