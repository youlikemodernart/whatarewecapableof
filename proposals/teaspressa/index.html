Prepared for Teaspressa / April 2026

Thirteen thousand wholesale accounts, built over eleven years through trade shows and founder energy. The distribution is real. The systems behind it were added one at a time, as the business grew. From the outside, the gap between the two is visible.

Teaspressa is a Phoenix-based CPG brand. Allison DeVane founded it in 2015, owns 100% of the company, and manufactures the products in-house. The Luxe Mixer Cubes (flavored sugar cubes for cocktails, lattes, and mimosas) are the main product. She appeared on Shark Tank early on, closed physical retail locations during COVID, and pivoted to wholesale. The collabs page states the business generates over seven figures through 13,000+ retail accounts.

We looked at the public-facing infrastructure: the DTC Shopify store, the separate wholesale Shopify store, the content, the analytics tools, the DNS records, and the technology connecting them. Five things stood out.

Findings
01

The wholesale operation serves 13,000+ retail accounts through a separate Shopify store at wholesale.teaspressa.com. Pricing is login-gated. Products are displayed in case packs. There's a blog coaching retailers on seasonal merchandising. But there's no line sheet, no reorder system, no account-level tracking, and no way for a buyer to browse after a trade show without logging into a second e-commerce store. The wholesale store also includes a "Sip & Shop Demo Package" at $120-$130, which shows investment in retail activation. The gap is between the scale of the distribution and the tools supporting it.

02

The DTC site runs six analytics and marketing tools: GA4 (G-MY5C4WWCLQ), Hotjar (5041504), Microsoft Clarity (rx17nkfzlk), Facebook Pixel (805449808864249), Klaviyo (UYrTva), and Postscript (7548). Each one drops its own tracking script directly into the page. There's no Google Tag Manager connecting them, no shared data layer, and no unified event model. Six tools collecting data independently means six partial views of the same customer.

What's working

The product merchandising is sophisticated: 100+ collections organized by drink type, format, flavor profile, base spirit, caffeine level, occasion, and price point. Seasonal rotation keeps the catalog fresh. Product-specific landing page subdomains (champagnekit.teaspressa.com, mimosakit.teaspressa.com, drink.teaspressa.com) suggest campaign-level marketing with QR codes on packaging. The wholesale blog coaching retailers on seasonal prep is a differentiator most CPG brands at this scale don't offer. Premium partnerships include NetJets x Monaco Grand Prix and a Greenwood Brewery collaboration.

Company

Teaspressa was founded in January 2015 in Phoenix, AZ. Allison DeVane is the sole owner. She invented a patented tea process that brews like espresso (the origin product), then created the Luxe Mixer Cube format, which became the primary revenue driver. Products are manufactured in-house in Phoenix. The about page describes her as an inventor and scientist. She is a Tory Burch Fellow and a Titan 100 CEO.

The origin story: selling tea from a laundry cart in downtown Phoenix, landing a Shark Tank appearance shortly after, opening a retail store, closing retail during COVID, and pivoting to wholesale-first distribution. The collabs page states the business has grown to over seven figures with 13,000+ retailers.

The distribution reached a scale where the systems behind it matter. The wholesale ordering, analytics, retention tools, and event-based verticals were each added as the business needed them. The opportunity is to connect them.

Three phases, ordered by leverage. Phase 1 addresses wholesale infrastructure because that's where the majority of revenue comes from. Phase 2 builds DTC retention. Phase 3 connects the measurement layer.

Phase 1

Wholesale buyer experience

A digital line sheet and buyer portal running on top of the existing wholesale Shopify store. The line sheet is the tool that lives after the trade show: a retail buyer can browse the seasonal catalog, check pricing, and reorder without logging into a separate e-commerce store. It looks like the brand, works on a phone, and can be forwarded internally.

  • Line sheet pulling product data, images, and pricing from Shopify. Seasonal collections highlighted. Shareable link per account or per collection.
  • Account-level visibility: order history, reorder shortcut, seasonal catalog preview, new product alerts.
  • Post-show follow-up sequence: every buyer scanned at the booth gets the line sheet with a personalized note and a one-click reorder path.
  • Klaviyo integration for wholesale-specific email flows: new product launch, seasonal transition, restock reminders.
Phase 2

DTC retention and recurring revenue

Convert the dormant subscription infrastructure into a live product. The seasonal flavor rotation provides a natural subscription story: subscribers receive the new collection before it hits shelves. Pair this with self-service quoting for wedding and corporate verticals.

  • Subscribe-and-save on key products: monthly or quarterly Mixer Cube delivery, subscriber-exclusive seasonal flavors, easy skip/swap/cancel.
  • Wedding configurator: select cube flavor, packaging, quantity, personalization. Instant quote.
  • Corporate gifting builder: budget-based recommendations, bulk pricing tiers, branded packaging options, recurring annual setup.
  • LoyalTEA program audit: is the current points structure driving repeat purchases?
Phase 3

Measurement and analytics

Google Tag Manager as the container for all tracking. A data layer connecting Klaviyo, Postscript, GA4, and the ad pixels. DNS cleanup. Review platform consolidation. One view of customer behavior across DTC and wholesale.

  • GTM setup with server-side tagging for accuracy and privacy compliance.
  • Unified event model: add-to-cart, purchase, email signup, SMS opt-in, review submission, subscription activation tracked as coordinated events.
  • Consolidate review platforms (Judge.me or Stamped.io, not both). Migrate existing reviews.
  • TikTok audit: per-video performance analysis. Restructure the content strategy or reallocate the production effort.
  • SEO baseline: indexed page count, ranking keywords, organic traffic, blog content performance.

This report was produced by analyzing publicly observable infrastructure: extracting structured data from product pages, parsing XML sitemaps, reading every script and pixel on the homepage, querying DNS records, checking SSL certificate history, and pulling the wholesale store's product API. Everything cited here has a source.

An engagement follows the same method: understand the system before changing it. We'd start with an internal audit (GA4, Klaviyo, wholesale account data, trade show records), then build a roadmap grounded in what the data shows, then execute in phases with measurable checkpoints.
