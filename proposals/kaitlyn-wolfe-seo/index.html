We looked at kaitlynwolfe.com the way a search engine does: what the current site on Cargo can and cannot do, what moving off Cargo would change, and where the searches that bring in work actually live.

The short version

A strong site on a platform that caps its search

The studio site is well designed and the photography carries it. The thing holding back its search is mostly the platform underneath it. Cargo makes a beautiful portfolio and gives away little of what a search engine reads, and the site has no page built to answer the searches that actually bring in design and build work.

Layer one · staying on Cargo

What we can fix without moving

Some real wins do not need a new site. We would do these first, whatever you decide about the platform.

01

Claim and optimize the Google Business Profile

The single biggest local lever does not touch the website. We make sure the studio's Google Business Profile exists, is verified, and is built out, so the studio shows up in the map and local results for Scottsdale and Phoenix searches. This works on any platform.

02

Put a real address on the site

The contact page shows only forms right now: no address, no phone, no city or state on the page. Adding consistent name, address, and phone is a core local-search signal, and it is straightforward to add on Cargo.

03

Rewrite the titles and the one description

Every page shares a single, identical description. We rewrite the page titles and that description toward the words people actually search, like Scottsdale interior design and custom home remodel, instead of page names. Cargo's control here is limited, but the wording is worth improving.

04

Clean up what Google sees

The sitemap lists 173 URLs, more than 40 of which are navigation, footer, archive, and test pages with no reason to be in search. We strip those out and ask Google to drop them, so the real pages stand on their own.

That is a real tune-up, and worth doing. It also has a ceiling. The things that move a site like this the most, per-page descriptions and titles, canonical tags, real headings, image alt text, structured data, modern image formats, and fast loading, are not things Cargo can do. To get those, the site has to move. We have already built that, and it is Layer two.

The strategy

Where the search traffic actually is

A portfolio is proof, and it earns the work once someone is already looking. It is not what people search. Almost nobody types “southwest zen project” into Google. They type “Scottsdale interior designer,” “Paradise Valley custom home builder,” “Phoenix luxury remodel,” and they search the studio by name. The current site has no page built to answer any of those.

The work that captures that demand is a set of service and location pages: interior design, design and build, renovation, new construction, commercial, each tied to the places the studio works, each carrying the right local signals, each linking into the projects that prove it. The portfolio becomes the evidence behind those pages. This is the part that actually moves rankings, and it needs the platform from Layer two to exist.

The same approach extends to the rest of the family when you want it. Shop My Warehouse, Substrate, and Ground Floor carry the buying intent today, and the studio site can feed them. We would start with the studio and grow out from there.
