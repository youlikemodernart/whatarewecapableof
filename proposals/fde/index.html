Overview

Why this exists

01

Campus clubs give FDE a presence while future founders are still deciding what success means. Students are choosing career paths, mentors, habits, and communities before FDE is usually in the room.

02

FDE already has material that can carry a semester: the 8-week video series with J.D. Greear and Henry Kaestner, the language of faith and work, and the vision for entrepreneurs bringing their gifts to the altar. Campus clubs make that material a shared weekly practice.

03

Student ownership changes the feel of the work. A club run by students can become a campus movement. Outside programming usually lands as another extracurricular. The proposed model gives students real responsibility, with coaching and guardrails around them.

04

Local church partnerships keep the work grounded in each city. The campus club becomes a bridge between students, churches, entrepreneurs, and the national FDE network.

05

The first year produces 10 clubs and a repeatable launch playbook: how to choose a campus, recruit a student director, pair mentors, run gatherings, report to FDE, and hand the model forward.

What is already built

The starting point is strong: founder-level language, curriculum, a national entrepreneur network, local churches already connected to the movement, and a clear theological frame for work. The campus initiative gives students a place to practice the mission.

What we would want to confirm

The plan depends on the first 5 campuses having the right conditions: a credible student director, a local church partner, at least one entrepreneur mentor, and enough institutional openness to gather weekly. A conversation would identify the first campuses, define the student director profile, and set the reporting cadence FDE leadership wants.

Program

The program

Three leader development layers and five recurring touchpoints. The layers define what student directors receive. The touchpoints set the cadence for each campus.

Leader development model

Layer 1

Content

FDE's 8-week video series with J.D. Greear and Henry Kaestner runs in community. Students watch, discuss, apply, and test the ideas against actual leadership decisions on campus.

Layer 2

Opportunity

Students lead weekly gatherings, semester events, and campus invitations. They practice leadership with real people in the room.

Layer 3

Coaching

Student directors meet every two weeks with local and national Faith Driven Entrepreneurs: church FDE graduates, Christian business owners, and people who have carried faith through operational pressure.

Operating principle

FDE provides mission, curriculum, network, and guardrails. Students carry the campus expression. That division gives FDE consistency without turning the work into a centralized program.

Mission

FDE's stated value is a "ruthless fervor to advance the mission for God's glory, not our own." The campus initiative gives students a place to practice that value before they start companies.
