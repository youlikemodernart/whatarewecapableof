Where the platforms miss

Georgie recommended ArtCloud because it matches what a lot of galleries at your scale use. Two things about both platforms make them weak fits for this specific goal.

01

Neither has a real API. Data goes in through Excel or CSV files and comes out the same way. The AI can prepare the file, but a person still imports it. After the initial load, every update runs the same loop: export, edit, import. That is the loop this system is meant to avoid.

02

Neither platform stores collector intelligence at the depth you described: relationship notes, passed-on works, fair context, and outreach timing. Connecting a Paris conversation to a piece coming back on consignment takes a data model built to your practice.

System

Architecture

Airtable holds the records. A web app at a BELHAUS subdomain provides the daily interface. An AI agent reads and writes via the API and sends nothing without you. Four integrations connect to Artsy, email, the image pipeline, and the existing Squarespace store.

Data model

Five connected tables in Airtable.

TableWhat it holds
ArtworksArtist, title, year, medium, dimensions, edition, provenance, pricing, consignment terms, current location, status, exhibition history, images.
Collectors and contactsName, email, phone, shipping address, cities, source, artists they collect, fairs met at, price range, purchase history, passed-on history, referral source, conversation notes, last contact date.
Exhibitions and fairsName, dates, location, artists, works shown, works sold, collectors attended.
ConsignmentsWorks in and out of BELHAUS, terms, dates, contact.
RelationshipsLinks between people, between collectors and artists, and between artworks and collections.

Relationships table

The Relationships table is a connective index for the other four tables. Each relationship record connects two things the gallery already tracks.

Relationship recordConnects
Artist interestPerson + artist
Inquiry, purchase, or passed-on workCollector + artwork
Exhibition or fair contextArtwork + exhibition or fair
Consignment pathArtwork + consignment
Spouse, advisor, referral, familyPerson + person

The fields are yours. Adding a new field takes a minute. As your practice changes, the schema changes with it.

The AI agent

The agent reads from Airtable, drafts into the web app, and sends nothing without you. It has three jobs.

Prepare

Records from sources

New artwork coming in: the agent drafts the record from whatever source you hand it, such as an invoice, a photo, or an email from the artist. You approve. New collector met at a fair: the agent creates the profile from a business card photo or a voice memo. You approve.

Surface

Collector intelligence

Before a fair, the agent pulls the relevant collector list and drafts outreach for each. Mid-conversation, it surfaces the last five pieces a collector asked about, who they asked about them with, and what they bought last. Quarterly, it flags stale relationships with next-step suggestions.

Draft

Copy in your voice

Press releases, social captions, SEO descriptions, artist spotlights, and private catalog blurbs. The agent produces a draft in your voice. You edit. Whatever you do not want drafted, it does not draft.

The agent proposes; you decide. Sending emails, changing pricing, and publishing content require your approval.

What's portable

The data model uses standard field names. If you ever want to move to Artlogic or ArtCloud, a one-time export matches their schema. You own the records, the images, and the history. Nothing's locked in.

Approach

Three phases

The first produces a populated database. The second puts the full system in daily use. The third extends capabilities based on what proves useful.

Build path

Phase 1 · 1 week

Data audit and initial population

Before any system goes up, we find out what data exists and what's missing. Decisions made early: what counts as a collector versus a contact, how consignment terms get stored, and which artwork fields matter most. Output: a starting dataset, complete where complete, flagged where incomplete.

Phase 2 · 4 to 5 weeks

Build the core system

The schema goes into Airtable, populated from the Phase 1 dataset. The web app ships as a functional v1: search, view, edit, log new records, private catalog generation. The agent comes online with core capabilities: prepare new records, surface collector intelligence, draft outreach.

Phase 3 · Ongoing

Extend based on use

Capabilities come online as they prove useful: private catalog analytics, outreach sequences, press release and social drafting, cross-business reporting with Aftermarket's Square data, and email newsletter migration off Squarespace Campaigns if it matters.
