What the first project handles

01

Summit and Ember need a product model that can hold many colors without turning the product page into a giant variant chooser. The working recommendation is one product per color or texture, with size as the main variant.

02

The master sheet should own product data. Shopify should publish approved records. That keeps pricing, sizes, MOQs, descriptions, and image choices tied to a known source.

03

Updated supplier pricing creates a margin review step before anything goes live. Kaitlyn and Chelsea should see proposed sell prices, margin, exceptions, and products that need judgment.

04

The image work matters because tile is a visual product and the current Substrate presentation is controlled. Manufacturer images can be useful, but they need selection, formatting, naming, and approval before upload.

05

The buyer path needs a decision before the build finishes. Some products may remain direct checkout. High-end or semi-custom tile may work better with a quote-first flow, especially where MOQ, lead time, or square-foot calculation affects the order.

What's already in place

The team has the important inputs: product knowledge, supplier references, a master sheet, physical samples, installed imagery, Shopify infrastructure, and defined internal roles around product processing and margin approval. The project turns those pieces into one controlled path.

Source of truth

The master sheet owns the product data. Shopify becomes the publishing layer. Existing Shopify records still matter, but they should be pulled and compared against the sheet before updates are written.

Default posture: dry run first, draft products before publish, approved pricing before price writes, and theme changes kept separate from product data changes.

Catalog flow

Step 1

Source packet intake

Collect the master sheet, updated costs, image links, Shopify access, margin rules, launch timing, and the team contact who can route questions.

Step 2

Normalization

Map sheet columns into a stable product dataset: titles, handles, SKUs, sizes, box square footage, cost, sell price, MOQ, lead time, collection, image URLs, and approval status.

Step 3

Pricing review

Calculate proposed prices and margins, flag exceptions, compare against existing Shopify records where relevant, and prepare a review file for Kaitlyn and Chelsea.

Step 4

Image preparation

Pull approved manufacturer images and internal images, match them to product colors, prepare consistent crops, and create contact sheets when image choice needs review.

Step 5

Shopify draft build

Generate dry-run payloads first. After approval, create draft products or approved updates with variants, images, collections, tags, and product copy.

Step 6

QA and launch handoff

Compare Shopify output against the master sheet, review desktop and mobile product pages, confirm quote or checkout behavior, and leave the team with a launch checklist and decision log.

After-launch support

After the first build, light month-to-month support can keep the catalog stable as the team starts quoting, selling, and adjusting the new line. That support can cover pricing corrections, product-data changes, Shopify questions, small product-page updates, and diagnosis when the team sees how buyers use the catalog.

Item Structure
Support model Optional month to month, with no long-term commitment.
Suggested range $350 to $500/month, depending on the support volume the team wants covered.
Larger requests Pre-approved separately at $110/hour or quoted as a fixed add-on.

Source packet request

The fastest way to start is to send the source packet in one shared folder and name the person on the team who should route questions. Raw source files should stay unchanged. Working copies can be created after intake.

Needed What to send or confirm
Shopify access Collaborator access for products, variants, collections, files or images, and theme read access. Theme write access only if quote-flow or calculator changes are approved.
Master sheet The current Substrate master sheet, with the canonical tabs or columns identified.
Updated costs Supplier cost list if separate from the master sheet, including any freight, handling, landed-cost, or MSRP notes that affect pricing.
Margin rules Target margin, minimum margin, exception rules, and who approves exceptions.
Image rule Manufacturer image-use permission or the team's rule for which source images are approved.
Launch timing Target date, sample box timing, packaging status, and marketing dependencies.
Buyer path Whether new tile should support direct checkout, quote-first behavior, or a mixed path. Also confirm what should happen to the calculator.
Team contact The person Noah should work with day to day for Shopify access, sheet questions, image review, and QA feedback.
Known discrepancies Any products where the team already knows the current site, Shopify backend, or visible copy has wrong pricing or product data.

Working rules

  • The master sheet owns product data.
  • Shopify publishes approved records.
  • Dry runs happen before writes.
  • New products stay draft until approved.
  • Existing-product updates are batched and approved before writing.
  • Theme changes are separated from product data changes.
  • Manual changes need to be captured in a decision log so future updates do not erase the reason for the change.
