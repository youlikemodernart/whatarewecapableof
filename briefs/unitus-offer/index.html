Unitus: Immanuel launch operating layer

A one-page internal offer outline for turning Unitus's launch workstreams into one reviewed weekly cadence.

Thesis

WAWCO operates the connective layer for the Immanuel 1 launch so Unitus's separate workstreams run as one reviewed weekly cadence instead of routing through John.

Current read

Treat the following as current understanding from one call, not a settled diagnosis.

  • John is rebuilding Unitus from close to a clean slate after ending prior agency and personnel relationships. He carries both the top leadership and CMO load on a lean team, and wants a future CMO the business is not ready to hire yet.
  • Several pieces are moving in parallel: a site rebuild going live shortly, a Klaviyo migration for email and SMS targeted before the end of June, a paid media partner change at the end of June, footwear production, prior-line inventory cleanup, and the Immanuel 1 launch itself.
  • Each piece can work on its own and still fail as a system because no one owns the connections or the weekly loop. John described survival mode and too much work falling back to him.
  • John is open to AI-assisted systems that produce draft work under human review. He holds a firm line against fake people, and he does not want to be the person prompting tools and assembling outputs.

Proposed WAWCO lane

WAWCO becomes the temporary operating layer for the launch and the growth loop that follows it. The role is operator: WAWCO builds and runs the connected systems and hands John reviewed outputs to approve.

The posture is operated systems and reviewed work product. John wants the work done and approved, not a diagnostic to read or an AI workflow to run himself.

What the lane includes:

  • One source of truth for launch data, assets, dates, and decisions.
  • A weekly scorecard and decision cadence across traffic, conversion, email and SMS, paid media, inventory, and offer.
  • Production workflows for landing pages, email, and SMS, built and run by WAWCO, with John or a named approver signing off before anything ships.
  • An AI-assisted data-to-action loop: pull the data, surface what changed, propose the next move, route it through a review gate.

What we would build first

First two weeks, scoped as a paid intake and launch map:

  • Source intake. Shopify data, paid media summary, Klaviyo migration scope, new site preview, product economics, launch calendar, creative assets, and the current email and SMS plan.
  • Launch system map. People, tools, decisions, assets, data, and dates in one place, including who already owns what, so WAWCO fills gaps rather than stepping on current contractors.
  • Launch objective and metrics. The Immanuel 1 launch objective and the metrics that define a good launch.
  • Weekly review format. A cadence built on John's loop: wins, losses, and opportunities for improvement.
  • First production workflow. Email and SMS or landing pages, with the review gate built in.

90-day shape

Framed around objective, execution, and reflection, tied to the launch calendar.

Weeks 1 to 2
Intake and launch map.
Weeks 3 to 8
Coordinate the preheat and presale path; build landing pages tied to paid media journeys; run the Klaviyo content workflow with a QA checklist; stand up the weekly scorecard; run the weekly review; build an internal source library so AI outputs stay grounded in brand guide, product facts, dates, and approved voice.
Weeks 9 to 12
Support launch iteration and post-launch reflection; turn what worked into reusable playbooks; decide whether WAWCO stays as the operating layer, hands off to a future CMO, or narrows to a system-build role.

What we need to learn before proposing

  • What does John need decided before the end of June?
  • What parts of the launch are locked versus still fluid?
  • Which contractors are committed, what do they own, and where are the gaps between them?
  • What data does John trust today across Shopify, paid media, email and SMS, inventory, and margin?
  • What would John hand off first if WAWCO started now?
  • How should AI outputs be reviewed before they reach customers?
  • What would make a 90-day engagement worth renewing or expanding?

Why this is the right WAWCO-shaped offer

  • The launch is a forcing function with a real date and a clear target. It scopes the work without trying to fix the whole company.
  • The gap John described is coordination and weekly ownership, which is what an operating layer provides.
  • It fits John's own framework: define the win, run the cadence, protect the mission. WAWCO supplies objective, execution, and reflection without adding to John's load.
  • It matches John's AI posture: systems that produce reviewed draft work, operated by WAWCO and approved by John. No fake people, and no prompt-and-assemble work landing back on him.

Boundaries and review gates

  • WAWCO maps current ownership before proposing any direct execution. Avoid stepping on the site builder, the Klaviyo migration, the new paid media group, the content team, or any creative lead.
  • Human review on everything customer-facing. John or a named approver signs off on copy, faith language, and brand voice before it ships.
  • No broad account access until there is a clear engagement, scope, and approval path. Exports or screenshots are enough for the first pass.
  • Treat all call details as private prospect context. Keep them out of public or client-facing material without review.
