Sophie Saint Motors: first drop

A small, well-made cotton knit drop, produced in LA, to seed the brand before the first real collection.

The opportunity

Sophie Saint Martin restores vintage cars, teaches people how, and is building a brand around bringing more women into the automotive world. Her restoration videos reach into the millions, consistently. Sophie Saint Motors is that world: the restoration shop and content brand, with its site launching this month (Caroline Ridings is handling the design, and made the introduction).

This first run is merch, not the main line. It exists to put the brand on people, give Sophie something to wear in her videos, and seed the feel of the world before her first real collection. A separate women's workwear line, Workwell (coveralls, gloves, thermals), is planned within about a year; if the merch patterns work, they can be carried over and adjusted for that launch.

The look

Vintage workwear and garage-girl: a fitted, early-2000s racing-tee feel. Think heather grey, white, and red; ringer tees, a henley or placket with a small chest patch, clean racing graphics. Fit is the thing Sophie cares about most (she loves the way Brandy Melville fits), so we build to fit first.

Reference pieces Sophie sent

These are now saved with the project and should drive the first sample conversations with Oscar. The reference garments are fit and construction evidence first, then graphic direction.

1. Ringer tee reference

Make the waist slimmer and the body more fitted. Useful details: cap sleeve, deep notch/placket, contrast coverstitch, worn-in varsity graphics, and a cropped garage-girl proportion that still needs production-friendly fit control.

Production note: fit slimmer through the waist; keep the notch/placket and contrast stitch language available for graphics and trim discussion.

2. Armless tank reference

Add about 2 inches of length so it covers the belly button. Reduce the flare at the bottom and keep it fitted. The reference is useful for armhole depth, high neck, clean body, and close torso fit.

Production note: lengthen the body, control the sweep, and test armhole comfort in the first fit sample.

3. 3/4-sleeve knit reference

Sophie called this a strong fit. Explore fabric options: the original is a thick knit, but a thinner knit, soft rib, or cotton tee fabric may work better for cost and wearability. Remove front and back patches, keep patches on the arms, and add Sophie Saint Motors logo placement similar to the other examples.

Production note: keep the arm-patch idea, remove front/back patches, and solve fabric after Oscar confirms construction and yardage.

Her standard

Sophie is CAD literate and comfortable reviewing technical sketches, fit notes, and colorways. The Workwell "Racing Coverall" deck is a good read on the standard and taste we're producing toward, even though the merch itself is simpler.

How we'll make it

  • Made in LA, small-batch cut-and-sew. At ~400 units on a 6-week clock, with a high fit bar, this is a domestic job, not overseas.
  • Built from pieces she already owns. Sophie has reference garments whose fit she loves; those become the patterns, with our modifications.
  • Through Oscar in LA, first. Sophie has an LA maker, Oscar, who can sew if we supply the fabric, buttons, and trims. Noah calls him Monday to confirm scope, pattern and yardage needs, hardware, and sampling. The rule stays simple: he sews one sample of the hardest piece before we commit the run.
  • Fit first. A first sample, a fit check, and a final approved sample before anything is cut at volume.
