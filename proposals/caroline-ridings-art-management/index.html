Building the business layer around your painting practice.

The goal is simple: you keep your attention on the work, and I build the operating system around it: management terms, business setup, artwork records, commerce, sales operations, and growth.

The short version

I would act as art manager and business-operations lead

I would help turn the painting practice into a working business without taking control away from you. I can prepare the systems, coordinate the setup, build the catalog, plan the store, and manage the operating work, but filings, accounts, payment routing, public listings, spending, sends, and signatures still need your separate approval.

The working compensation structure is a 22% manager share on covered art sales. The agreement defines how that base works, what is excluded, and how payment gets handled.

I also prepared a separate Artist Management and Limited Business Authority Agreement for signature. That document controls the authority and payment terms if it conflicts with this proposal.

Build SEO, Pinterest, press, collector, gallery, email, and paid-media plans only after the commerce and reporting layer can handle real demand.

Authority

Clear approval gates before anything risky

The agreement gives me authority to prepare and manage the work. It does not give me a general power of attorney, and it does not let me sign for you.

I can prepare

Drafts, checklists, plans, product records, metadata, research, workflows, proposed messages, account setup materials, and private operating documents.

I can coordinate

Nonbinding information, quotes, support, troubleshooting, and setup questions with platforms, vendors, galleries, professionals, or support teams.

You approve first

Filings, account openings, payment routing, platform terms, public listings, pricing, customer sends, third-party commitments, refunds, shipping, subscriptions, ad spend, physical custody, and any signature for you.

Sensitive info

Sensitive information comes only after there is a secure intake plan. It should not be sent by ordinary text, email, or chat.

Compensation and payments

Make the math checkable

The agreement uses a 22% manager share for covered art sales during the term, with exclusions for pre-existing commitments and adjustments for refunds, chargebacks, uncollected sales, taxes, pass-through shipping, and approved discounts.

For direct sales, the base is the artwork sale price actually collected after those exclusions. For gallery, Artsy, BELHAUS, marketplace, or other third-party sales, the base is what you receive after that third party takes its commission or platform fee.

The preferred payment architecture to evaluate later is Stripe Connect or a similar setup, if the platforms support it and it stays clean. The fallback is a monthly statement and manual settlement. No live payment routing is configured by signing the agreement.

Ownership

You own the core assets

The default is that you or your entity own the LLC, EIN, bank account, Stripe or Shopify Payments account, Shopify store, domain, customer list, artwork records, public channels, and primary image archive.

I can hold admin or operator access where needed, but access should be documented, limited to the work, and revocable. I keep my reusable templates, systems, automations, code, workflows, prompts, reporting formats, and operating methods. You own the artist-specific content and records made for your business.

What I need first

A small set of starting answers

  • Which works are available, sold, reserved, committed to a gallery or show, or not for sale.
  • The first 5 to 10 works you would want to prepare for launch.
  • Whether prices should be public, price on request, or mixed.
  • Whether the painting commerce site should stay separate from your creative-direction portfolio or connect to it.
  • Whether you already have any LLC, EIN, seller permit, bank, Stripe, Shopify, domain, bookkeeping, or Pinterest setup.
  • Any existing gallery, collector, commission, Artsy, BELHAUS, or other commitments that should be excluded.

Sensitive information comes later, after we approve the secure intake method.
